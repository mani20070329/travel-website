Essential Tips for Your Trip

Before You Go

  • Check Visa Requirements: Make sure to check if you need a visa to enter Indonesia.
  • Travel Insurance: Consider getting travel insurance to cover any unexpected situations.
  • Currency Exchange: Familiarize yourself with the local currency (Indonesian Rupiah) and exchange rates.

During Your Stay

  • Respect Local Customs: Bali has a rich culture, so be mindful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress Modestly: When visiting temples, wear appropriate attire (sarongs are often provided).
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially if you're exploring outdoors.

Transportation Tips

  • Use Ride-Sharing Apps: Grab and Gojek are popular options for getting around.
  • Rent a Scooter: If you're comfortable, renting a scooter is a great way to explore.
  • Be Cautious: Traffic can be hectic; always wear a helmet and drive safely.

Health & Safety

  • Stay Safe: Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Vaccinations: Consult with a healthcare provider regarding recommended vaccinations.
  • Emergency Numbers: Keep emergency contact numbers handy (e.g., local police, ambulance).

Enjoying Local Cuisine

  • Try Local Dishes: Don't miss out on traditional dishes like Nasi Goreng and Babi Guling.
  • Street Food: Sample street food but ensure it's from a reputable vendor to avoid foodborne illnesses.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink bottled water, especially if you're not used to the local water supply.
